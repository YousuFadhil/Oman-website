    Season 13
    The Wilayat of Ibra is one of the wilayats of the North A’Sharqiyah Governorate. It is bordered on the north and east by the Wilayat of Al-Mudhaibi, on the south by the Wilayat of Al-Qabil, and on the east by the Wilayat of Dima and Al-Ta’ayyin. It is about 180 km away from the capital of Muscat. It is the administrative center in North A’Sharqiyah, and it took the Aqaba Mosque as its emblem. Its population centers are about 65, including: Ibra, Al-Nassib, Al-Thabti and Al-Hamdi. The state's land is covered with ophiolite rocks and sedimentary rocks that contain various minerals, and its nature varies between desert, mountains, sand dunes, valleys and water springs. Among the valleys are: Wadi Qafifa, Wadi Al-Mazrou’, Wadi Al-Maleeh, Wadi Warid, and its water springs: Ain Abu Saleh, Ain Al-Dhabyan, Ain Shabihat, Ain Al-Maleh, and Ain Al-Khab. There are many mountainous and wild plants in Ibra, such as Sidr, Al-Samar, Al-Ghaf, Al-Qart, Al-Suqam, Al-Arsh, Al-Jada, Al-Shakaa, Al-Dhafra, and Al-Saql. Ibra includes many historical sites, such as: Al-Zahir Castle, Al-Shabak Fort, Frefer Fort, Al-Daghsha Fort, Al-Ain Fort, Al-Qurain Fort, Al-Qutbi Tower, Al-Nasiri Tower, Sana’a Tower, Al-Mansour Tower, the Citadel Tower, Al-Natala Tower, Al-Safah Tower, Al-Qurun Tower, Al-Manzafah Wall, Al-Qanatir Wall, Al-Alayah Wall, Sohar Wall, Al-Siyahuah Wall, and Beit Al-Majdara, Al-Yahmadi House, and Al-Qasimi House. Among its archaeological sites is Al-Manzafah Quarter, which contains ancient buildings, inscriptions and decorations, and the site of Al-Jubaila, in which circular tombs and archaeological sites for copper extraction were found. Archaeological sites were also discovered in its valleys, such as: Wadi Ibra, Wadi Al-Batha and Wadi Al-Athly, which date back to the third millennium BC. There are also old markets in the state that have been vanished, including: the market of Imam Azzan bin Qais (reigned: 1285-1287 AH / 1868-1871 AD), the market of Haret al-Qanater and the al-Yahmadi market. It is also famous for the Wednesday market, which is a weekly one for women. Among its most famous ancient mosques is the Imam Azzan bin Tamim Mosque (reign: 277-280 AH / 890-893 AD). The residents of the Wilayat of Ibra engage in economic activities, such as: agriculture, trade, blacksmithing, carpentry, and grazing, in addition to craft industries, such as: textile, palm leaf, and leather industries, and silverware such as: daggers and jewellery, construction tools, metal industries, and sugarcane industry. Among the musical arts practiced by its residents: Al-Razha, Al-Azi, Al-Mahbal, Al-Qasafiyah, Al-Tashah, Al-Taghrood, Al-Tareq, Al-Wana, Al-Midan, Al-Zar and Al-Hadi. Its farmers rely on aflaj to irrigate their crops. As there are about 54 falajs, of which 31 are alive.

    Season 12
    The Wilayat of Dima and Al-Tayyin is one of the wilayats of the North Al Sharqiyah Governorate. It is located between the Eastern Hajar Mountains. It is bordered to the north by the Wilayat of Qurayyat, to the south by the Wilayat of Ibra, Bidiyah and Al-Qabil to the east, and Al-Mudhaibi and Bidbid to the west. It is about 170 km from the capital, Muscat. Its population centers are about 112 localities, including: Mahlah, Al-Ghubeira, Al-Khutm, Al-Hajer, and Ismailia. Its nature varies between mountains, caves, valleys, and eyes, and its natural features are: the White Mountain, Bohban Cave, Wadi Dayqah, Ain Al-Murr, Ain Al-Sukhna, Ain Al-Misfah, Ain Ajma, Ain Al-Ajf, Ain Al-Tannery, Ain Tamah, Ain Maqta’a, and Ain Al-Zarqa. Its residents depend on the falaj for irrigation and daily uses, as it has about 224 falaj, of which 187 are alive. The state includes a number of historical sites, such as: the fort of the town of al-Hamam, the fort of the town of Khubba, and the fort of the town of al-Hosn, and its history is associated with a number of personalities such as: Hilal bin Said Ibn Arabah (live in: 1273 AH / 1857), and Saif bin Hamoud al-Batashy (d.: 1420 AH / 1999 AD). ). Its residents practice several economic activities, including: agriculture, grazing, blacksmithing, pottery and jewelry. It is famous for breeding Arabian horses, and among the musical arts that are practiced in it are: Al-Razha, Al-Azi and Al-Zaffah.

    Bidiyah is one of the states of Al Sharqiyah North Governorate. It is located in the center and is bordered to the north by the wilayat of al-Qabil, and to the east by two states: al-Kamil and al-Wafi, and Wadi Bani Khalid. Its population centers are about 106 localities, including: Al-Muntarib, Shihak, Al-Hawiyah, Al-Wasel, and Al-Zahir. The nature of Bidiyah varies between sands, mountains, plains, and green oases, which number 15 and are dotted with wild plants. There are sands of the Sharqiyah, and it also includes water springs, including: Ain Abu Suhaila, Ain Abu Sarimah, Ain Abu Ghafah, Ain Habsin, Ain Yaya, and Ain Al-Tamr. As there are 17 falajs, of which nine are live falajs, and all of them are falajs of Udayyah. Wild trees such as Ghaf and coniferous trees are widespread in the Wilayat of Bidiyah, and there are types of wild animals such as deer, in addition to reptiles and birds. Bidiyah includes historical sites such as: Al-Wasel Castle, Al-Muntarib Fort, Al-Shariq Fort, Al-Hawiyah Fort, and Al-Ghabi Fort. The Wilayat of Bidiyah in the Al Sharqiyah North Governorate has tourist and heritage attractions and unique local and external fame that have contributed to enhancing its natural assets and unique environment. Bidiyah and its beautiful oases with soft golden sands represent a transit gateway for convoys of foreign tourists and visitors heading to the Sharqiyah sands.     Season 10
    The Wilayat of Al Mudhaibi is one of the states of the North Al Sharqiyah Governorate. It is bordered on the north by the wilayats of Bidbid, Samail, Ibra, and Al Qabil. On the south, it is bordered by the Wilayat of Mahut. From the east, it is bordered by three states: Bidiyah, Jalan Bani Bu Hasan, and Jalan Bani Bu Ali. On the west, it is bordered by the Wilayat of Izki and the Wilayat of Adam. to her . Its population centers are about 262 localities, including: Al-Mudhaibi, Sinaw, Samad Al-Shan, Khadra Bani Difa and Al-Rawdah. The nature of the state varies between mountains, deserts, oases, caves, valleys, and water springs. Among its mountains: Al-Rawdah Mountains and Jabal Madra, in which there are several caves, and its valleys: Wadi Samad, Wadi Al-Rak, Wadi Kharma, Wadi Ataka, and among the water springs: Ain Al-Hareed. The residents of the wilaya depend on the falaj for irrigation, as there are about 156 falaj in it, and there are many different types of wild trees, such as Sidr and Ghaf, as well as some wild animals such as deer. The Wilayat of Al-Mudhaibi includes archaeological sites such as: Al-Mayasar site, and the Andam Valley site, in which settlements dating back to the third millennium BC were found along the gravel terraces on the banks of the Andam Valley. Graves were also found in the village of Muhaliya dating back to the last period of the Iron Age. Among the historical monuments in Al-Mudhaibi: Al-Rawdah Fort in the town of Al-Rawdah, and Khuzam Fort, which is located in the Niyabat of Samad Al-Ashaan on a small hill in the western bank of Wadi Samad and is surrounded by palm trees on several sides, and the Bait Al-Khabib Fort, which was built on a mountain in Haret Al-Khabib. The residents of the wilaya engage in a number of economic activities and crafts, such as: agriculture, the manufacture of agricultural tools and palm fronds, and the manufacture of Omani sweets.

    Season 9
    The Wilayat of Al-Qabil is one of the states of the North Al-Sharqiyah Governorate. It is bordered by the Wilayat of Ibra on the north, from the south by the Wilayat of Bidiyah, and from the west by the Wilayat of Al-Mudhaibi. It has about 69 archaeological monuments, the most important of which is a fort in Al-Qabil and another in Al-Mudayrib. It is also famous for the presence of a number of villages located among the high golden sand dunes, the most important of which are: Saqqa-Al-Aqidah-Khurais-Al-Jawfa. There are also a number of springs in the state, the most important of which are: Ain Marzouk, known for its mineral waters, Ain Wadi Baraka, Ain Al Washal. Al-Aflaj, Al-Ayoun and the villages located in the middle of the sand dunes are among the tourist attractions of the Wilayat of Al-Qabil. The Wilayat of Al-Qabil is one of the symbols in the Omani history that has penetrated since ancient times. Therefore, it is distinguished by its ancient heritage. The forts, castles and towers that abound in the Wilayat are but the best evidence of its status. It is located in the eastern part of the Al Sharqiyah North Governorate on the main road between the cities of Muscat and Sur, bordered by the Wilayat of Wadi Bani Khalid from the eastern side and the Wilayat of Bidiyah from the southern side, and from the north it is bordered by the Wilayat of Ibra and from the northeast it is bordered by the Wilayat of Wadi Al Damma and Al Tayyin, while from the west it is bordered by the Wilayat of Al Mudhaibi. The state is characterized by the diversity of terrain between sand dunes, high mountains and low valleys. The state is also distinguished by a long heritage and the presence of ancient archaeological buildings, in addition to the presence of towers that were built a very long time ago, estimated at hundreds of years, and which have their stature and lofty status, yet they are still steadfast, defying all days, years, erosion factors, and terrain.

    Season 8
    The Wilayat of Wadi Bani Khalid is one of the states of Al Sharqiyah North Governorate. It is bordered to the north by the Wilayat of Dima and Al Tayyin, to the south by the Wilayat of Al Kamil and Al Wafi, to the east by the Wilayat of Sur and to the west by the Wilayat of Bidiyah and Al Qabil. It took Wadi Bani Khalid as its emblem. The Wilayat includes some valleys such as Wadi Bani Khalid, from which the Wilayat gained its name. It is a continuous flowing valley with a length of 49 km. It passes through a number of villages, including a few and Mqil. It includes ponds and springs: Ain Hamouda, Ain Al Sarooj and Ain Dawa. The Mukl Cave is located north of the valley. There are about 63 falajs in the state, of which 62 are alive. The Wilayat of Bani Khalid includes historical sites such as: Al-Mawalik Fort, Al-Adfeen Fortress, Al-Raziqiyyin Fortress, Al-Awaina Fort and Al-Awaina Mosque. It is considered the Mawalek fort, which is the largest fort in the state, and its construction dates back to the fourth century AH, and it was a center for the governor and judge in the past. The state's residents engage in economic activities: agriculture, grazing, livestock breeding, blacksmithing, palm-tree manufacturing, weaving, gold and silver industry, and its musical arts: Al-Razha, Al-Azi, Al-Midan, Al-Sharh and Abu Zulf.
